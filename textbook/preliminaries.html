Mathematic preliminaries¶

Typical prerequisites for statistics and econometrics are:

  • linear algebra
  • calculus
  • probability

They usually take 2-4 semester in college. Linear algebra is fully covered by VMLS, and probability is exposed in PSC, even though latter is a compact reference, not formally a textbook. Scipy lectures is a great one-stop resource for numerical computing basics. I do not have a one single source to recommend for calculus yet.

Linear Algebra¶

  • Vectors, Matrices, and Least Squares (VLMS)

Probability and statistics¶

  • Probability and Statistics Cookbook (PSC)
  • Probability and Statistics for Engineers and Scientists (PSES)

Numerical computing¶

  • Scipy lectures: one document to learn numerics, science, and data with Python
